# Diet plan for Foz do Iguaçu (Brazil): low cost, low volume, high protein, high nutrient density

## 1) Assumptions and how calories are set (using lower ends)

Because you did not provide sex, height, and weight, the safest way is to prescribe by **body weight (kg)** and use conservative (lower-end) activity factors.

- Activity profile used: mostly on feet at work + occasional HIIT/resistance training.
- Lower-end maintenance estimate used: **31 kcal/kg/day**.
  - Practical range for your profile is often ~31–35 kcal/kg/day; we use **31** as requested.
- Target deficit: **500 kcal/day**.
- Optional conservative deficit: **100 kcal/day** (included because you mentioned it once).

### Calorie formulas

- **Maintenance** ≈ `31 × bodyweight(kg)`
- **Cut target (500 deficit)** ≈ `31 × kg - 500`
- **Light cut target (100 deficit)** ≈ `31 × kg - 100`

### Protein/fat/carbs (high protein emphasis)

Use the lower end for calories, but not for protein quality:

- **Protein:** `2.0–2.2 g/kg` (aim 2.1 g/kg)
- **Fat:** `0.6–0.8 g/kg` (floor 0.6 g/kg)
- **Carbs:** fill remaining calories

This keeps protein high while preserving hormonal health and training support.

## 3) Food strategy in Foz do Iguaçu (cost + low volume + nutrient quality)

Prioritize cheap, nutrient-dense, high-protein foods available in Brazilian markets (Muffato, Condor, Atacadão, etc.).

### Core low-cost staples

1. Eggs
2. Chicken breast/thigh (buy in family packs)
3. Sardine (canned in water/tomato) and/or tuna
4. Beans (small portions, for micronutrients/fiber)
5. Rice (moderate portions for training days)
6. Oats (small amount)
7. Milk powder or plain yogurt (when on promotion)
8. Soy protein (PTS) as a budget protein booster
9. Seasonal vegetables (especially dark leafy + orange veggies)
10. Banana/papaya/orange (rotate by price)

### Low-volume rules (important for your request)

- Build each main meal around **30–45 g protein** first.
- Use modest carb servings (80–130 g cooked rice or equivalent).
- Use calories from eggs/olive oil/peanut paste when needed without increasing volume too much.
- Keep beans and salads to functional amounts (not huge bowls).
- Prefer denser fruits (banana) when appetite is low.

## 4) Practical daily meal template (for 70 kg example at ~1670 kcal)

This is a 500-deficit sample day. Adjust by bodyweight formulas above.

### Meal 1 (pre-work or breakfast)
- 3 whole eggs
- 30 g oats with water/milk
- 1 banana

Approx: ~430 kcal, ~24 g protein

### Meal 2 (lunch)
- 180 g cooked chicken
- 100 g cooked rice
- 80 g cooked beans
- 1 portion vegetables + 1 tsp olive oil

Approx: ~560 kcal, ~50 g protein

### Meal 3 (snack)
- 170 g plain yogurt (or 250 ml milk)
- 20 g peanut paste (or peanuts)

Approx: ~250 kcal, ~12 g protein

### Meal 4 (dinner)
- 1 can sardines (125 g drained) or 150 g chicken
- 150 g potato or cassava (cooked)
- Vegetables

Approx: ~420 kcal, ~32 g protein

### Daily total (example)
- **Calories:** ~1660–1700 kcal
- **Protein:** ~118–130 g

For a strict 70 kg protein goal (~147 g), add one of:
- +120 g chicken (adds ~35 g protein), or
- +30 g soy protein/PTS (adds ~15 g protein) + 2 egg whites.

## 5) Ultra-budget version (still high protein)

Use this when prices are tight:

- Base proteins: eggs + chicken thigh + PTS + sardines (2–4x/week)
- Carbs: rice + beans + cassava
- Micronutrients: carrots, cabbage, pumpkin, kale, bananas, oranges

Target structure:
- 4 eggs/day
- 250–350 g chicken/day (raw weight equivalent)
- 30–50 g PTS/day
- 1 can sardine every other day

This usually beats whey in BRL per gram of protein.

## 6) Micronutrient coverage checklist

Hit these each day/week:

- **Calcium:** 1–2 servings dairy (or fortified alternatives)
- **Iron + folate:** beans + leafy greens + vitamin C fruit
- **Omega-3:** sardines 3–4 cans/week
- **Potassium/magnesium:** banana, beans, leafy greens
- **Iodine:** iodized salt (small amounts)
- **Vitamin D/B12:** eggs, fish, sunlight routine

## 7) Training day vs rest day tweaks

- **Training days:** add 20–40 g carbs near workout (banana/rice/potato).
- **Rest days:** remove that carb and keep protein unchanged.
- Keep average weekly deficit at 500 kcal/day.

## 8) How to adjust every 2 weeks

Track:
- Morning body weight (3–4 times/week average)
- Energy during work shift
- Gym performance
- Hunger and sleep

Adjustments:
- If weight loss is >0.9% bodyweight/week and fatigue is high: add +100–150 kcal/day.
- If weight is stable for 2 weeks: subtract -100 kcal/day.
- Never reduce protein to force calories lower.
